Most Asked Questions

When selecting a crane, consider the following:

Load Capacity: Ensure the crane can handle your heaviest loads.
Lift Height and Reach: Match the crane's capabilities to your operational needs.
Environment: Consider if the crane will operate indoors, outdoors, or in harsh conditions.
Maintenance and Service: Choose a manufacturer with reliable after-sales support and quick response times.
Customization: Some manufacturers offer tailored solutions to meet specific industry requirements.

Safety and reliability are paramount. Look for cranes that comply with industry standards and regulations, such as ANSI, OSHA, or ISO certifications. Manufacturers should provide detailed safety features, including overload protection, emergency stop functions, and regular maintenance programs. Additionally, check the manufacturer's track record for reliability and customer testimonials.

After-sales support is crucial for maintaining your crane's performance. Expect the manufacturer to offer:

Warranty: A comprehensive warranty covering parts and labor.
24/7 Service: Rapid response times for breakdowns or urgent repairs.
Spare Parts Availability: Easy access to genuine parts to minimize downtime.
Training: Operator and maintenance training programs to ensure safe and efficient use of the crane.
Regular Inspections: Scheduled inspections to ensure ongoing compliance and safety.
